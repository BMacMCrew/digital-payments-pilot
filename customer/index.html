Real-World Use Cases

See how Actus solves critical payment challenges across DHS components

FEMA Emergency Supplies

RTP Focus

Challenge:

Rapid purchase of generators and water during disaster response - payment needed within hours

Solution:

Real-Time Payments (RTP) provide instant, irrevocable funds so suppliers ship immediately

The Flow:

  1. Buyer creates PO in Actus (CGAC 070, TAS)
  2. 3-way match passes for confirmed items
  3. Actus generates SPR → ERP → Treasury PAM approves
  4. Fiserv RTP executes: DHS sponsor bank → supplier bank (settled in seconds)
  5. Webhook updates Actus → line items marked Paid

Impact:

Faster disaster response deployment with real-time visibility for finance teams

CBP Software License

G2B Virtual Card

Challenge:

Pay SaaS vendor that accepts cards but isn't RTP-enabled

Solution:

G2B virtual cards backed by stablecoin - no sponsor bank dependency required

The Flow:

  1. PO validated in Actus → SPR sent to Treasury PAM
  2. After approval, Actus requests one-time virtual card via Fiserv G2B
  3. Vendor processes card normally through Visa/Mastercard
  4. Stablecoin converts to fiat → supplier merchant account
  5. Webhook updates reconciliation in Actus

Impact:

Broad supplier coverage using existing card infrastructure - no special onboarding needed

TSA Multi-Vendor Equipment

Atomic Split

Challenge:

One PO covering equipment (Vendor A), installation (Vendor B), and platform fee

Solution:

Atomic settlements ensure all-or-nothing execution across multiple parties

The Flow:

  1. SPR totals $X across multiple parties
  2. Treasury PAM approves total amount
  3. Actus triggers Fiserv atomic block: Vendor A, Vendor B, and fee settle together or none do
  4. Webhook records per-party settlement with single audit reference

Impact:

Financial integrity across multi-party disbursements - prevents partial payment failures

Key Terms

Essential terminology for understanding Actus Digital Payments

SPR - Standard Payment Request
The official file format agencies submit to Treasury's PAM system for payment processing. Contains PO, invoice, supplier, CGAC/TAS, and compliance evidence.
PAM - Payment Automation Manager
Treasury's system that receives, validates, and certifies SPRs before funds can move. Ensures obligations and budget authority are correct.
BFS - Bureau of Fiscal Service
Treasury's bureau that ingests certified payment requests, validates them, and determines the disbursement channel (ACH, Fedwire, FedNow).
CGAC/TAS Codes
Common Government Accounting Classification (CGAC) identifies the agency (DHS = 070). Treasury Account Symbol (TAS) identifies the specific budget line.
3-Way Match
Verification that Purchase Order = Invoice = Receipt confirmation before payment is approved.
Atomic Settlement
All-or-nothing execution across multiple parties on the blockchain. Either everyone gets paid exactly as specified, or no one gets paid. Prevents partial payment failures.
RTP / FedNow
Real-time instant bank-to-bank payment rails. RTP is run by The Clearing House, FedNow is operated by the Federal Reserve.
G2B Virtual Card
Government-to-Business stablecoin-backed, one-time virtual card issued via Fiserv. Supplier sees a standard Visa/Mastercard payment - no special onboarding required.
Smart Contract
Automated compliance rules built into the blockchain. Example: IF (PO approved AND invoice valid AND goods received) THEN generate SPR and submit to Treasury PAM.
Blockchain Orchestration
Actus coordinates multi-party settlements via blockchain distributed ledger that manages multiple FedNow gateway payment rails while ensuring immutability and compliance.
