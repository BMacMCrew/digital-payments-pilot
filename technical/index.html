🎯 Actus Strategic Position

Actus operates as the Data Entry / Payment Creator within the Treasury SPS dual-authorization model. We create complete, pre-audited, blockchain-backed Standard Payment Requests (SPRs) that federal Certifying Officers then review and cryptographically sign. This preserves the two-person rule while dramatically improving efficiency and accuracy through automation and immutable audit trails.

Key Differentiator: Actus functions as an OMB A-123 internal controls engine, providing cryptographically verifiable proof of compliance at every step from commitment to disbursement.

System Architecture

Actus provides the blockchain orchestration layer that bridges agency financial management systems with Treasury payment execution systems. Operating as intelligent middleware, Actus translates on-chain events into actions within traditional federal financial management systems and vice versa, ensuring every step is cryptographically verified, synchronized, and immutable. Our platform automates the coordination of disparate systems, processes, and compliance checks required to execute a government-to-business payment from commitment through final settlement.

🏦 Capitalizing on the Open Banking Revolution

Actus's strategic position leverages the open banking revolution to facilitate unprecedented accountability, compliance, and transparency in federal fund disbursement. By managing Commitments, Obligations, Expenses, and Disbursements through scalable immutable ledger systems, we bridge critical gaps between agency financial management systems and Treasury payment rails.

Core Components Explained

1. Smart Contract Escrow & Commitment Management

  • Creates cryptographically secured escrow commitments at contract award
  • Manages obligation locks on portions of escrowed commitments
  • Enforces budget authority and anti-deficiency protections
  • Releases obligations only upon successful invoice matching and receipt verification

2. OMB A-123 Internal Controls Engine

  • Our key differentiator: Automated enforcement of OMB Circular A-123 principles
  • Immutable audit trail providing verifiable proof of compliance
  • Embedded regulatory rules prevent improper payments before execution
  • Enforces separation of duties through blockchain-based dual authorization
  • Real-time compliance monitoring and exception reporting

3. SPR Builder & Validator (Data Entry Role)

  • Assembles complete Standard Payment Requests from PO, invoice, receipt data
  • Validates CGAC/TAS codes against Treasury account structure
  • Performs automated 3-way match (PO = Invoice = Receipt)
  • Generates immutable blockchain transaction linking all source documents
  • Creates pre-audited package for Certifying Officer review

4. IPP/WebView Integration Gateway

  • Enables agencies without integrated financial management systems to use Actus
  • Creates SPRs directly through Bureau of Fiscal Service WebView interface
  • Bridges Invoice Presentation Portal (IPP) for invoice submission
  • Maintains blockchain immutability even for manual entry workflows

5. Compliance Engine

  • Enforces Federal Acquisition Regulations (FAR/DFARS)
  • Validates financial obligations and budget commitments
  • Checks anti-deficiency protections automatically
  • Audits all transactions for Payment Integrity Information Act (PIIA) indicators
  • Generates compliance reports for CFO Act audits

6. Blockchain Ledger

  • Stores immutable records from appropriation through disbursement
  • Creates cryptographic proof chain for all payment lifecycle actions
  • Enables atomic settlements across multiple parties and systems
  • Provides real-time audit trail accessible to Treasury and agency CFOs
  • Supports continuous monitoring for OMB A-123 compliance

7. FedNow & ISO 20022 Integration

  • Constructs ISO 20022 messages (pacs.008, pacs.002) from blockchain events
  • Ensures payment instructions are traceable to authorizing source
  • Enables real-time gross settlement (RTGS) for instant payments
  • Maintains immutable linkage between on-chain events and FedNow transactions

8. Atomic Settlement Engine

  • All-or-nothing execution for multi-party payments
  • Coordinates across FedNow, RTP, ACH, and card networks
  • Guarantees financial integrity - no partial failures or reconciliation gaps
  • Immutable settlement records prevent disputes

G2B Payment Lifecycle

This workflow demonstrates Actus's orchestration role from commitment through settlement.

Complete Payment Flow

  1. Smart Contract Creation & Escrow Commitment
    Agency action (contract award, budget commitment) triggers Actus to create a smart contract on blockchain. This establishes a cryptographically secured escrow commitment of funds with immutable record of obligation authority.
  2. Obligation Management with Locks
    Purchase orders and other instruments create specific locks on portions of escrowed commitments. Obligations remain locked on blockchain until release conditions are met.
  3. Invoice Matching & Obligation Relief
    Vendor submits invoice through IPP or direct integration. Actus performs automated 3-way match (PO = Invoice = Receipt of Goods/Services). Successful match relieves the obligation and triggers SPR generation.
  4. SPR Creation (Data Entry Role)
    Actus assembles complete Standard Payment Request with all supporting documentation, blockchain proofs, and compliance validations. This pre-audited package is prepared for Certifying Officer review.
  5. Certifying Officer Review & Signature (Dual Authorization)
    Designated federal Certifying Officer independently reviews the SPR package and supporting blockchain evidence. Officer cryptographically signs using PIV-based digital signature in Treasury SPS, certifying the payment's legality and accuracy.
  6. SPS Processing & PAM Generation
    Treasury Secure Payment System receives certified SPR and generates Payment Authorization Manager (PAM) file. This triggers Bureau of Fiscal Service payment processing.
  7. Atomic Settlement Coordination
    Actus coordinates with Fiserv gateway, FedNow, and blockchain to execute atomic settlement. The entire multi-step transaction either completes successfully across all systems or fails entirely, preventing partial states or reconciliation gaps.
  8. Immutable Settlement Record
    Final settlement is recorded on blockchain with cryptographic proof of execution. All parties receive real-time webhook notifications with immutable transaction references.

⚠️ Critical: Dual-Authorization Model

Actus NEVER performs the certification step. We create the payment request (Data Entry role), but a separate, designated federal Certifying Officer must independently review and sign (Certification role). This separation of duties is legally mandated and cryptographically enforced.

Integration Guide

🔌 Critical Integrations for Market Readiness

The following integrations are mandatory requirements for Actus to provide complete G2B payment orchestration:

  • 100% Fiserv Gateway Capability - REQUIRED for payment execution across all rails
  • 100% WebView Interface Capability - REQUIRED for SPR creation in Treasury systems
  • FedNow Service Integration - MUST COMPLETE for real-time ISO 20022 messaging
  • IPP Portal Integration - REQUIRED for agencies without integrated financial management systems

ERP Integration Flow

Actus provides seamless integration with major federal ERP systems through standardized connectors and APIs.

  1. Purchase Order Creation: Agency creates PO in their ERP system (SAP, Oracle, Momentum, etc.)
  2. Data Synchronization: Actus receives PO data through secure integration layer with real-time synchronization
  3. Smart Contract & Escrow: Actus creates blockchain commitment and locks obligation
  4. Invoice Receipt: Supplier submits invoice (via IPP, direct integration, or WebView for non-integrated agencies)
  5. 3-Way Match: Actus validates PO = Invoice = Receipt using OMB A-123 controls
  6. SPR Generation (Data Entry): Actus creates blockchain-backed SPR with all compliance evidence
  7. Certifying Officer Review: Federal official independently reviews and signs in SPS
  8. PAM Generation: Treasury BFS generates Payment Authorization Manager file
  9. Payment Execution: Actus triggers Fiserv/FedNow for atomic settlement
  10. Webhook Notification: Settlement confirmation updates ERP through secure integration

Supported ERP Systems

  • SAP: Direct API integration with real-time data sync
  • Oracle EBS: Web services integration with automated workflows
  • Momentum: Batch file exchange with webhook callbacks
  • Legacy Systems: SFTP/CSV import with validation layer

IPP/WebView Integration (Non-Integrated Agencies)

For agencies without direct ERP integration, Actus provides gateway access to Treasury's systems:

  • Invoice Presentation Portal (IPP): Submit invoices through BFS portal with blockchain recording
  • WebView Interface: Create SPRs directly through Treasury's WebView with Actus orchestration
  • Manual Entry Workflow: Maintains blockchain immutability even for manual processes
  • Compliance Preservation: Same OMB A-123 controls apply regardless of entry method

Security & Compliance

FedRAMP Authorization (Baseline Requirement)

FedRAMP-Authorized Environment

Actus operates within a FedRAMP-authorized infrastructure. This is a baseline security standard for any cloud service handling federal data, not an optional certification.

  • Authorization Level: FedRAMP Moderate baseline implemented
  • Infrastructure: Deployed on FedRAMP-authorized cloud service providers
  • Reciprocity: Agencies can leverage existing authorization to accelerate ATO
  • Continuous Monitoring: Ongoing compliance validation and security assessments

Authority to Operate (ATO) Process

The ATO is granted by a federal Authorizing Official (AO), never by a contractor. For systems connecting to Treasury payment rails, the Treasury/BFS AO holds ultimate authority.

Paperless Innovations Responsibilities:

  • Provide complete System Security Plan (SSP)
  • Maintain Control Implementation Summary (CIS)
  • Document architecture and data flows
  • Implement NIST 800-53 Moderate/High baseline controls (421 controls)
  • Support security assessment process
  • Provide continuous monitoring reports

Treasury/Agency AO Responsibilities:

  • Review and approve SSP and security documentation
  • Conduct Security Assessment & Authorization (SA&A)
  • Grant formal ATO (typically 3-year authorization)
  • Review audit logs and compliance reports
  • Approve Interconnection Security Agreements (ISAs)

Authentication & Access Control

  • JWT Tokens: All API requests require valid JWT bearer token
  • Token Expiry: 1 hour default, refresh tokens valid 30 days
  • Scopes: Fine-grained permissions (read:payments, write:spr, certify:payments)
  • PIV Integration: Support for PIV-based authentication for Certifying Officers
  • Role-Based Access Control: Enforces separation of duties (Data Entry vs Certification)

Data Encryption

  • In Transit: TLS 1.3 for all API communications
  • At Rest: AES-256 encryption using FIPS 140-3 validated modules
  • Blockchain: Cryptographic signatures (ECDSA) for transaction integrity
  • Key Management: Hardware Security Modules (HSMs) for key storage

OMB A-123 Internal Controls

Automated Compliance Enforcement

Actus embeds OMB Circular A-123 requirements directly into the blockchain workflow:

  • Dual Authorization: Cryptographically enforced separation between Data Entry and Certification
  • Receipt of Goods/Services: 3-way match verification before obligation relief
  • Invoice Matching: Automated validation of PO = Invoice = Receipt
  • Payment Approval: Certifying Officer signature required before execution
  • Immutable Audit Trail: Complete blockchain record for IG and GAO audits

Audit Logging & Accountability

  • Immutable Logs: Write-once, time-stamped records on blockchain
  • Retention: 7-year retention for federal audit requirements
  • SIEM Integration: Real-time feeds to Treasury security monitoring
  • Non-Repudiation: Cryptographic signatures prevent transaction denial
  • Incident Reporting: 1-hour notification to Treasury for security incidents

Payment Card Industry (PCI) Compliance

Virtual Card Security

For G2B virtual card payments:

  • Never store raw PANs - use Actus tokenization
  • Implement hosted payment fields for sensitive data capture
  • Log card usage but mask all but last 4 digits
  • Fiserv gateway handles PCI DSS compliance for card processing

Blockchain Security

  • Consensus: Byzantine Fault Tolerant (BFT) algorithm
  • Nodes: Distributed across Treasury, agencies, and Actus infrastructure
  • Immutability: Cryptographic hash chaining prevents retroactive modification
  • Smart Contract Permissions: Role-based execution control
  • Private Blockchain: Permissioned network for federal use only
